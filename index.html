About Family Creative Learning

To take charge of their learning, young people must have access to a supportive learning ecology that includes peers, family, and educators. Family Creative Learning is a series of worksohps that engages parents and their children in learning together through their use of creative technologies. The workshops are collaboratively designed with community centers that serve communities with limited access and social support around computing.

Engaging in making

Parents and their children engage in design-based activities using creative tools, like Scratch and MaKey MaKey. They collaborate to create and invent their own personally meaningful projects.

Learning to learn together

As they create together, families learn how to support one another in their learning. For example, with their curiousity and savvyness with technology, children can support their parents in using the creative technologies, while parents support their children's learning by asking quesitons and providing encouragement during frustration.

Building connections

In addition connecting within their own family, parents and children connect with other families in the workshop, by sharing meals from local restaurants, engaging in inter-family activities, and showing their projects to each other at the end of every workshop.
